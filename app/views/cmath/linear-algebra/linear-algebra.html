The [1,1/4] consists of two column vectors, and it has very close relationship with the right hand side of matrix which is [10].
As we'll see later, for more complex linear system, we may see if the system has a solution or not by just analyzing the column spaces.

The null space, on the other hand, is all about the relationships between column spaces rather than the right hand side matrix.
As we can see, the null space we got was [1,-4], it tries to solve the 2nd equation of (1) whatever combination of (x,y). Actually, the ratio is 1:4 and to make it 0, we need to make it cancel, and that's why we have 1 and minus -4.

Whenever we use null space, the column space always comes up, which may trick us to think the two are similar.
So, the more we focus on the similarities between the null space and the column space, the more we'll be confused. However, they are totally different spaces in terms of their purposes, and to grasp the clear concept of those two spaces, we should be more focused on the differences of the two:

  1. null space describes the relationship between column spaces but not interested in the RHS matrix.
  2. column space has direct links to RHS, and tries to match RHS matrix.


In terms of dimension:

  1. null space can have maximum dimension as big as the number of columns of a matrix.
  2. column space can have maximum dimension as big as the number of rows of the matrix.
